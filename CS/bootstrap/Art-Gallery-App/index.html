The Mona Lisa

The Mona Lisa by Leonarso da Vinci is a likely a portrait of the wife of a Florentine merchant. For some reason however, the portrait was never delivered to its patron, and Leonardo kept it with him when he went to work for Francis I, the King of France. The Mona Lisa's mysterious smile has inspired many writers, singers, and painters.

The starry night

This mid-scale, oil-on-canvas painting By Vincent Van Gogh is dominated by a moon- and star-filled night sky. It takes up three-quarters of the picture plane and appears turbulent, even agitated, with intensely swirling patterns that seem to roll across its surface like waves.

The Scream

Edvard Munch's "The Scream" is a haunting depiction of existential angst. The painting's distorted figure and vivid colors evoke a profound sense of anxiety and despair, resonating with viewers on a deeply emotional level.
